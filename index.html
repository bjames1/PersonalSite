About Me

I am a researcher at the intersection of data science and psychology, with a PhD in Biological Psychology and a Master's degree in Data Analytics and Applied Statistics. My work focuses on leveraging advanced statistical methods and data visualization techniques to understand complex behavioral and neurobiological patterns.

My research interests include computational neuroscience, predictive modeling in behavioral science, and the application of machine learning techniques to psychological research. I am passionate about transforming complex data into meaningful insights that advance our understanding of human cognition and behavior.
